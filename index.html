Scene Flow Estimation:

A Survey








01  Introduction

Scene flow describes the 3D motion of the world, which is one the of most fundamental properties of the scene. In this paper, we revisit the nature of scene flow estimation by raising the three questions:

  • What is scene flow?

  • Why do we need scene flow?

  • Why is scene flow estimation under-developed?

    • “One representation of the scene motion is therefore a dense three-dimensional vector field defined for every point on every surface in the scene.”

    Sundar Vedula

    “Movement is an inherently continuous process. The relative motions between objects in a scene and cameras, and between different moving objects in the scene produce the apparent motions of objects between frames. The objective of motion analysis is to infer the real motion of objects in the scene from the changes between adjacent frames.”

    Zhengyou Zhang



    02  Overview

    Revised Definition: What Is Scene Flow?

    “Scene”: Real Motion of the Scene

    Scene flow represents the real motion of the scene, and hence, scene flow is not exactly the same as range flow or three-dimensional extension of optical flow. A key word for this issue is “ego-motion”.

    “Flow”: Continuous Nature beyond Discretization

    Scene flow describes a continuous function of the surface deformation, and current scene flow estimation is an approximation that discretize the scene flow spatially and temporally.

    04  Progress

    Besides the challenges for scene flow estimation, to answer the question of why scene flow estimation is under-developed, we need to understand what progress has been made over the years.

    As a maximum a posteriori (MAP) problem, we represent the modeling of scene flow estimation problem using the explicit factor graph, where the posteriori probability includes an unary term namely data term and two pairwise terms for spatial and temporal regularization. The different designs of these terms lead to diverse scene flow models, where the trend in each kind of term reveal the progress of modeling.

    Apart from the modeling, to solve the MAP problem, different optimization methods have been taken, along with some special implementations. We provide details about each category, and stress the trend in each period to depict the development of scene flow estimation.
